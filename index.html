Why I Chose to Code

I believe the answer comes down to being a problem solver at heart. To take a problem and develop a solution to completion is satisfaction in itself. The reason for inspiration surprisingly came from a commonly known YouTuber who goes by the handle of, "Techlead," and it all stemmed from a video he made about why he dislikes working at Google. This was around the time I was getting out of the Navy and figuring out the next step in my life so no option was off the table. I was just so stumped in wondering how someone could dislike working at Google that I couldn't help but watch. It turns out the entire video was a montage of hilarious satire. Regardless, I was hooked.

I will say though that the reasoning behind why was that I really wanted to work with people who thought similarly to Software Engineers and developers. I discovered a newfound passion for technology and the way the internet worked. To really dive into the history of software development excited me. I bought books, Udemy courses, started my CS degree, and even attended YouTube Univerity. I was all in. An interesting example of what I find amazing is how old JavaScript was. What's even better is learning it for the first time. I still can't believe the old school IDE was notepad. My thanks go out to Microsoft for developing VSC and I'm sure other developers feel the same!
